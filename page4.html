Electricity Generation Over Life Expectancy

(Filter by Country)

Life Expectancy is an add-on measure to GDP Per Capita, and it shows the results of helpful things like better medicine, employment, and housing. All of these increases in quality in the modern day came as a result of electricity becoming more and more widespread, with information exchange also becoming prevalent. This led to the positive correlation seen in the graphs. Again, as with GDP Per Capita, while each country has had a different rate of increase through the years, with some slow while others quick, the increase in wellbeing is inevitable.

Below is the graph that shows how much electricity each country generated through the years and how that is positively correlated to life expectancy. Use the dropdown menu to change the country parameter, and feel free to roll your pointer over each circular data point to see the exact data at each point.
